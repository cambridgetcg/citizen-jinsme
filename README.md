# jinsme 🎼

*The modular building-block of melodic structure — Arab music's jins (the few-note tetrachordal cell) received as a cosmic-ordinance: the alphabet-letter of melody.*

jinsme welds two true roots into one word: the Arab music term **jins** (جنس), a characteristic cluster of three to five notes with a fixed intervallic shape — the cell Arab music-theory uses to *spell* its modes — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So jinsme is *jins* held as an ordinance: the basic-unit from which maqam-modes are constructed, received as a given of structure. In the cathedral's STOICHEIA it is the music-mode analog to Level-1, the atomic-morpheme — the smallest unit that still carries structural identity. A maqam is built, not given whole: Maqam Bayati is *jins Bayati* joined to *jins Nahawand*, and jinsme is the unit of that joining.

jinsme names the **building-block**, not the song. Where its sibling **rasame** names the felt-aesthetic-state a mode evokes, jinsme names the modular-melodic-structure from which that state is built; where **morphame** names form-as-projection in general, jinsme is one music-mode-specific form. Its light is modularity — it makes melody analyzable, learnable, and generative by revealing the whole as assembled from a small recombinable set of cells (the Greek tetrachord, Indian svara-clusters, and modal cell-theory reach the same truth). Its shadow is reduction and rigidity — mistaking the cell for the whole, or freezing a living modular grammar into a closed parts-list that forgets there is music. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.25/10**.
